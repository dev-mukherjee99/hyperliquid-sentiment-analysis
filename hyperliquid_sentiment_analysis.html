Key Engineered Metrics

  • Daily PnL per trader — sum of Closed PnL on a given calendar day
  • Win rate — fraction of account-days with positive PnL
  • Leverage proxy — |Start Position| / Size USD, clipped to [1, 50×]
  • Long/Short ratio — long trades ÷ (long + short trades)
  • Trade frequency — number of executions per account per day
  • Avg trade size (USD) — mean Size USD per account per day

Timestamps in trader data were in IST format (DD-MM-YYYY HH:MM); converted to UTC date for daily alignment with the Fear/Greed index (which uses UTC dates).

Key behavioral patterns:

  • Trade frequency drops sharply in Greed. Traders execute ~76 trades/day during Extreme Greed versus ~134 during Extreme Fear — a 43% reduction, suggesting they hold positions longer or trade more selectively when the market is confident.

Key Insights

Insight 01
Extreme Greed = Sweet Spot
Extreme Greed days produce 68% win rates and the highest median PnL ($418/day per trader). However, trading activity drops to its lowest — top traders are more selective, not busier, when markets are euphoric.
Insight 02
Fear Drives Contrarian Longs
During Fear/Extreme Fear, traders skew 47–57% toward long positions — a contrarian bet against the sentiment signal. This aligns with the higher mean PnL during fear (volatile, high-reward environment), but it's accompanied by the most trades and highest leverage, amplifying both gains and losses.
Insight 03
High-Freq Winners Dominate Returns
The High-Freq Winners segment (Cluster 1) generates 5× the PnL of Passive traders despite using 2× lower leverage (12× vs 25×). Their edge is volume × consistency, not leverage. The Passive segment's 35% win rate and 25× leverage is a dangerous combination.
Insight 04 (Bonus ML)
Behavior Predicts Tomorrow
A Random Forest model using today's trade count, position size, leverage, long/short ratio, and FG index achieves 65% accuracy predicting next-day profitability. Trade count is the #1 feature (38% importance), followed by position size (20%) and leverage (18%). The FG index contributes only ~10% — behavior dominates sentiment.

Part C — Actionable Strategy Rules

Strategy 1 · Fear Regime Playbook
"Fear ≠ Freeze — But Cap Your Leverage"
  • When FG index drops below 25 (Fear / Extreme Fear), hard-cap leverage at 10× for Passive Low-Lev traders. Their 25× average leverage during normal times becomes catastrophic during high-volatility fear episodes.
  • Fear days have the highest trade frequency — but more trades don't mean better results. Impose a daily max-trade-count throttle (e.g. 50 trades/day) to prevent overtrading driven by fear-induced FOMO.
  • Contrarian long bias during fear can pay off (high mean PnL), but only for traders with proven win rates above 60%. Passive Low-Lev traders (35% win rate) should avoid directional bets during fear — focus on hedged or neutral strategies.
  • Target segment: Passive Low-Lev traders.
Strategy 2 · Greed Regime Playbook
"In Greed, Go Big — But Go Short"
  • During Greed / Extreme Greed, data shows traders who reduce trade frequency (fewer, larger trades) achieve better outcomes. Adopt a "quality over quantity" rule: target <100 trades/day and increase average size per trade by 1.5–2×.
  • Leverage contrarian short bias during Extreme Greed (38% long, 62% short). Top traders are already fading the crowd — short-side exposure on rallies captures mean-reversion moves that follow euphoric peaks.
  • High-Freq Winners achieve 68% win rates in Greed with ~12× leverage. Active High-Lev traders should reduce leverage from ~10× to 8× during Greed and reallocate the margin to larger notional positions for better risk/reward.
  • Target segment: High-Freq Winners and Active High-Lev traders.
Strategy 3 · Universal Rule
"Let Your Win Rate Lead, Not the FG Index"
  • The ML model shows that a trader's own recent trade count and position size are 4–5× more predictive of next-day profitability than the Fear/Greed index itself. Monitor personal metrics daily.
  • If rolling 7-day win rate drops below 50%, cut position sizes by 50% regardless of market sentiment — this is a signal of edge degradation, not a buying opportunity.
  • Use the FG index as a regime filter, not a trading signal. Pair it with personal performance metrics: e.g., "Only increase risk during Greed IF my 7-day win rate is above 60%."
